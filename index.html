Research Background & Motivation

Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) is a representative polymer material widely used in aerospace, chemical engineering, and precision manufacturing due to its excellent chemical stability, low friction coefficient, and high temperature resistance. However, compared with metallic materials, PTFE exhibits strong temperature sensitivity and significant elastic deformation during machining, which poses substantial challenges for achieving high-precision turning.

In practical PTFE machining, unstable chip formation and chip entanglement frequently lead to surface defects, dimensional inaccuracies, and reduced machining efficiency. Traditional cutting theories developed for metals fail to accurately predict the minimum uncut chip thickness of PTFE, motivating this project to establish a dedicated theoretical and engineering framework for polymer precision machining.

Research Objectives

  • Investigate the chip formation mechanism of PTFE under various cutting parameters
  • Establish a temperature-aware minimum uncut chip thickness model
  • Compensate elastic deformation to improve cutting accuracy
  • Design a negative-pressure chip removal device for stable machining

Methodology & Technical Approach

Chip Formation Analysis

An orthogonal cutting experimental platform was constructed to collect cutting force signals in real time. PTFE chips generated under different cutting conditions were observed using a VHX-700F digital microscope and scanning electron microscopy (SEM). Fractal dimension analysis was applied to quantify chip morphology characteristics.

Minimum Uncut Chip Thickness Modeling

Based on experimental observations, a PTFE-specific minimum uncut chip thickness theory was established. A temperature-dependent Johnson‚ÄìCook constitutive model was introduced to account for surface stress variations and elastic recovery during cutting.

Key Innovations

  • Temperature-dependent cutting model: Introduced temperature terms into the Johnson‚ÄìCook model to improve accuracy for polymer machining.
  • Elastic deformation compensation: Quantified elastic recovery after tool unloading to correct actual cutting depth.
  • Theory‚Äìengineering integration: Combined cutting theory with a negative-pressure chip removal device.

Engineering Implementation

A performance monitoring and control system was developed using an STM32 microcontroller and Python-based upper-computer software. The system supports real-time sensor data acquisition, dynamic visualization, and motor control for mechanical positioning.

Additionally, a negative-pressure chip removal device consisting of a visual inspection camera, air nozzles, vacuum generator, and chip suction module was designed and experimentally validated, significantly improving machining stability.
